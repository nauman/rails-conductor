Alerts & notifications

A proper event/alert pipeline — notify operators on deploy failure, app/container down, cert expiry, and failed maintenance, over per-org email and webhook channels with de-duplication.

Pillar: Continuous Maintenance Priority: P2 Effort: M Status: Planned

Problem

When a deploy fails, an app or container goes down, a TLS cert is about to expire, or a scheduled maintenance/audit job fails, operators currently find out by checking — opening the dashboard and noticing red. There is no proactive notification, no per-organization routing, and no way to send to Slack/Discord. Hatchbox notifies on deploy status out of the box; Conductor needs at least parity, plus the maintenance-oriented alerts that fit its "continuous maintenance" positioning.

Current state

The state needed to fire alerts already exists, but notification is thin and hard-wired. Deployment tracks status through STATUSES including succeeded / failed (app/models/deployment.rb); fail! already calls AlertMailer.deployment_failed(self).deliver_later (line 79), and Server#mark_offline! calls AlertMailer.server_offline only on the online→offline transition (app/models/server.rb:82–88). ContainerStatus#sync! (app/services/container_status.rb) already detects down containers — it sets container_status to exited/dead/unknown and flips App.status to stopped/failed (and App#needs_attention? in app/models/app.rb encodes "this is unhealthy") — but nothing emits a notification when that happens.

Delivery is email-only and global: AlertMailer (app/mailers/alert_mailer.rb) has just three actions — backup_failed, deployment_failed, server_offline — and every one sends to: admin_emails, i.e. User.admins.pluck(:email) across the whole instance, with no per-org scoping and no de-duplication. Email goes out over AWS SES SMTP, configured in config/environments/production.rb (delivery_method = :smtp, smtp_settings pointing at SES_SMTP_HOST / email-smtp.us-east-1.amazonaws.com with SES credentials, lines 63–76). There is no alerts/events model, no container-down or cert-expiry notification, and no webhook/Slack/Discord channel.

Proposed design

Introduce an event/alert pipeline that decouples "something happened" from "who gets told, and how." Sources emit typed ops events — deploy_failed, container_down, cert_expiring, audit_failed — each carrying a severity and a stable dedup key. An Organization configures one or more notification channels (email via the existing SES setup, plus Slack/Discord-style incoming webhooks). A dispatcher resolves the org's channels for an event, applies de-duplication and rate-limiting (e.g. one alert per container-down incident, not per status-sync tick), and delivers. Events are emitted from the points that already know the truth: Deployment#fail!, the container ContainerStatus status-sync path, and the cron/maintenance jobs. This generalizes today's hard-wired AlertMailer calls into routed, per-org, de-duplicated alerts while keeping SES as one channel among several.

Implementation slices

  1. Per-org notification channels: a model + UI for configuring email and webhook (Slack/Discord) channels on an Organization, with a delivery adapter per type (email reusing SES SMTP, webhook via HTTP POST).
  2. Event emission: define ops event types and emit them from the deploy path (Deployment#fail!) and the status-sync path (ContainerStatus / App#needs_attention?) for deploy_failed and container_down.
  3. Delivery + dedup/rate-limit: a dispatcher that fans an event out to the org's channels, with a dedup key + cooldown so a single incident notifies once, not on every poll.
  4. Maintenance alerts: add cert_expiring (from cert/TLS checks) and audit_failed (from the cron/maintenance jobs) event sources.

Acceptance criteria

  • A failed deploy (Deployment#fail!) emits a deploy_failed event that notifies the deploying app's organization on its configured channel(s).
  • A container transitioning to down (detected by ContainerStatus) alerts exactly once per incident — repeated status syncs while it stays down do not re-notify.
  • Channels are scoped per organization: an org only receives alerts for its own apps/servers, on the channels it configured.
  • At least two channel types work end to end: email via SES and an outgoing webhook (Slack/Discord).
  • Cert-expiry and failed-maintenance events fire alerts through the same pipeline.

Dependencies & related

Generalizes the existing AlertMailer (app/mailers/alert_mailer.rb) and the hard-wired calls in Deployment#fail! (app/models/deployment.rb) and Server#mark_offline! (app/models/server.rb). Consumes container-health signals from ContainerStatus (app/services/container_status.rb) and App#needs_attention? (app/models/app.rb), and relies on the SES SMTP delivery configured in config/environments/production.rb. Needs the org's cron/maintenance jobs as the source for audit_failed, and a TLS/cert check as the source for cert_expiring. Related: any rollback/auto-recovery work that might act on a container_down event.
